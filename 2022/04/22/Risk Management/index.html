Risk

Risks are an aspect of uncertainty.
A risk is an uncertain event or condition that, if it occurs, has a positive or negative effect on one or more project objectives.

  • Threats: Negative risks
  • Opportunities: positive risks

Risk Management

Project Risk Management includes the processes of conducting risk management planning, identifying, prioritizing, numerically analyzing, planning and implementing risk responses, monitoring on a project.

  • Plan Risk Management
  • Identify Risks
  • Perform Qualitative Risk Analysis
  • Perform Quantitative Risk Analysis
  • Plan Risk Responses
  • Implement Risk Responses
  • Monitor Risks
    The objectives of project risk management are to increase the probability and impact of positive risks and to decrease the probability and impact of negative risks, in order to optimize the chances of project success.

SWOT Analysis

SWOT(strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats) analysis is a framework used to evalute a company’s competitive position and to develop strategic planning. SWOT analysis assesses internal and external factors, as well as current and future potential.

  1. SO
  2. ST
  3. WO
  4. WT
    short-term, medium-term, long-term activities

Qualitative

Qualitative methods for risk assessment are relatively rapid in practice, cost effective and easily understood. The Result from the qualitative assessments are not an accurate estimate of risk.

Qualitative

Probability and impact matrix

Quantitative

Quantitative assessment methods provide more accurate analysis results than the qualitative assessment. However, they are costlier and often time consuming, so only the risk prioritized by the qualitative assessment are analyzed.
Quantitative analysis: Simulations, Sensitivity analysis, Decision tree analysis.

  • Simulations
    • Monte Carlo Technique
  • Sensitivity Analysis
    • Tornado Diagram
  • Decision Tree Analysis
